Road map to compete JEE exam.

March 07, 2024.
- Sachin Kadam
  • 800
  • 169

To compete effectively in the NEET exam, it's essential to focus on key topics across Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. Here's a breakdown of important topics within each subject:

Physics: Mechanics (Kinematics, Laws of Motion, Work, Energy, and Power) Thermodynamics and Kinetic Theory of Gases Electrostatics and Current Electricity Optics and Optical Instruments Modern Physics (Atomic Structure, Dual Nature of Matter, Nuclei, Semiconductor Devices) Waves and Sound

Chemistry: Basic Concepts of Chemistry (Atomic Structure, Periodic Table, Chemical Bonding) Organic Chemistry (General Organic Chemistry, Hydrocarbons, Haloalkanes and Haloarenes, Alcohols, Phenols, and Ethers) Inorganic Chemistry (Coordination Compounds, p-Block Elements, d- and f-Block Elements) Physical Chemistry (Thermodynamics, Chemical Equilibrium, Chemical Kinetics, Solutions) Environmental Chemistry Solid State, Solutions, Electrochemistry

Biology: Diversity in Living Organisms (Classification, Five Kingdom Classification, Plant Kingdom, Animal Kingdom) Structural Organization in Animals and Plants (Morphology, Anatomy, and Anatomy of Flowering Plants) Cell Structure and Function (Cell Theory, Cell Organelles, Cell Division) Plant Physiology (Photosynthesis, Respiration, Plant Growth and Development) Human Physiology (Digestive System, Circulatory System, Respiratory System, Excretory System, Nervous System, Endocrine System) Genetics and Evolution (Principles of Inheritance and Variation, Molecular Basis of Inheritance, Evolution)

Other Important Topics: Biomolecules (Carbohydrates, Proteins, Lipids, Nucleic Acids) Reproduction in Plants and Animals Ecology and Environment Human Health and Diseases Biotechnology and its Applications

Zoology: Human Physiology: This lesson delves into the study of the functioning of various systems in the human body, such as the digestive system, respiratory system, circulatory system, excretory system, nervous system, endocrine system, and muscular-skeletal system. Animal Diversity: This lesson covers the classification and characteristics of different groups of animals, including sponges, cnidarians, flatworms, roundworms, segmented worms, arthropods, mollusks, echinoderms, and chordates.

Genetics and Evolution: This lesson explores the principles of inheritance, including Mendelian genetics, chromosomal theory of inheritance, DNA replication, transcription, translation, genetic disorders, population genetics, mechanisms of evolution, and evidence for evolution.
Reproduction: This lesson focuses on the reproductive systems of animals, including humans, and covers topics such as gametogenesis, fertilization, embryonic development, reproductive hormones, menstrual cycle, contraception, reproductive health, and reproductive strategies in various organisms.

While focusing on these topics, ensure a deep understanding of fundamental concepts, as questions in NEET can be conceptually challenging. Also, prioritize topics based on your strengths and weaknesses, allocating more time to difficult areas while revising regularly to reinforce your understanding. Additionally, solving ample practice questions and mock tests focusing on these topics will help in better preparation and confidence-building for the exam.
